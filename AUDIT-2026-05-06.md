## Executive Summary

**Overall Health Score: 7.5 / 10**

Citeback is in solid pre-launch shape. The architecture is principled, the security headers are exemplary, the privacy claims are largely accurate, and no tracking scripts exist anywhere in the codebase. The pre-launch messaging is honest and consistent. The technical build is clean.

However, two issues require immediate attention before any public community-facing promotion:

### Top 3 Issues

1. **🔴 CRITICAL: ICANN Domain Verification Deadline** — citeback.com must verify domain ownership via `citeback@proton.me` by **May 14, 2026** (8 days away). Missing this deadline suspends the domain entirely. Check `scotthughes070@proton.me` for the verification email now.

2. **🟠 HIGH: TrustFAQ Contains Stale Netlify Forms Text** — The "What data does browsing this site expose?" FAQ answer states that form submissions "are processed and stored by Netlify Forms, including any contact information you provide and your IP address." This is factually wrong — forms route to Hetzner (`ai.citeback.com`) with IP logging disabled, and no contact info is collected. This directly contradicts the Privacy Policy. Any visitor who reads both documents will notice the conflict.

3. **🟠 HIGH: Privacy Policy and Terms of Use Not Discoverable** — `/privacy` and `/terms` pages exist and are well-written, but neither is linked from the Nav or Footer. Users can only reach them by typing the URL directly. For a platform making strong privacy claims, this is a legal exposure: if users can't find the Terms, the OFAC disclosure and donation terms within them have limited enforceability.
