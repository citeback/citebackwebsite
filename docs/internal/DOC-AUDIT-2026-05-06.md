# DOC-AUDIT-2026-05-06.md — Citeback Fee Model Change Manifest

**Audit type:** Read-only. No changes made.
**Auditor:** Subagent (citeback-doc-audit)
**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Purpose:** Identify every location where the OLD funding model (graduated 3–5% platform fee) appears and specify exactly what must change under the NEW model (zero fee / pure pass-through / voluntary tips).

---

## OLD MODEL (docs currently reflect)
- Graduated 3–5% platform fee on campaign donations
- Platform receives fee at disbursement and routes it to operations wallet
- Fee schedule: 5.0% up to $10k, 4.5% $10k–$25k, 4.0% $25k–$50k, 3.0% above $50k
- All tiers apply to rolling 90-day operator volume
- Operations wallet covers VPS, TEE, Monero node infrastructure

## NEW MODEL (pending final decision)
- Zero platform fee on campaigns
- Pure pass-through: donor → TEE wallet → vendor direct (no platform cut)
- Voluntary tips to LLC (separate, user-initiated — not deducted from campaigns)
- Scott covers ops via capital contributions
- GOVERNANCE.md fee schedule section needs to be replaced or removed

---

## ⚠️ Audit Note: PRE-LAUNCH.md §6 Is Itself Stale

PRE-LAUNCH.md item #6 states: *"ARCHITECTURE.md references '1% platform fee' in two places — Actual fee is graduated 3–5% per GOVERNANCE.md §7.3."*

However, the current ARCHITECTURE.md does **not** say "1%". It correctly shows the graduated 3–5% schedule in both locations (Design Principles #5 and the Wallet Agent Fee Model table). This means **the fix described in PRE-LAUNCH.md #6 was already applied to ARCHITECTURE.md** but PRE-LAUNCH.md was never updated to mark the item complete. PRE-LAUNCH.md item #6 is stale and should be checked off or deleted.

This audit is flagging it for awareness but it does not change the scope of the new-model migration below.

**Reference 1**
- **Location:** §7 Campaign Goals and Platform Fees (full section)
- **Exact quote:**
  ```
  ### 7. Campaign Goals and Platform Fees

  **Platform fees:** The Platform charges a graduated fee on campaign funds as follows:
  - 5.0% on rolling 90-day operator volume of $0–$10,000
  - 4.5% on rolling 90-day operator volume of $10,001–$25,000
  - 4.0% on rolling 90-day operator volume of $25,001–$50,000
  - 3.0% on rolling 90-day operator volume above $50,000

  These fees are deducted automatically by the Platform's TEE-based financial system at disbursement. Fees support Platform infrastructure and operations. Fee calculation is based on rolling 90-day operator volume, not per-transaction.

  **Campaign goals include a fee buffer:** Published campaign goals include a 2% buffer for Monero transaction fee variance. This buffer is disclosed transparently on each campaign card.

  **The operations wallet is publicly visible** via published Monero view keys. Any operations wallet surplus beyond six months of projected operating costs is subject to community governance vote for redistribution.
  ```
- **What it currently says:** Defines platform fees (graduated 3–5%) as a legal term, including automatic deduction at TEE disbursement, operations wallet funding, and fee buffer on campaign goals.
- **What it should say under new model:** REWRITE — see note. Replace §7 with:
  ```
  ### 7. Campaign Goals and Donations

  **Platform fees: None.** The Platform does not charge a fee on campaign donations. All funds raised flow directly to the campaign operator/vendor at disbursement (pure pass-through).

  **Voluntary tips:** Donors may optionally tip the Platform Operator (Wyoming DAO LLC) to support infrastructure costs. Tips are separate from campaign donations, user-initiated, and are not deducted from campaign funds.

  **Platform operations** are funded by founder capital contributions and voluntary tips. The Platform does not take a cut of campaign funds.

  **Campaign goals include a transaction fee buffer:** Published campaign goals include a small 2% buffer for Monero network transaction fee variance. This buffer is for network fees only — it is not a platform fee.
  ```

## Additional Notes for Whoever Makes the Cuts

**1. The voluntary tip mechanism doesn't yet exist anywhere in the codebase.** Every rewrite above gestures at it ("voluntary donor tips, user-initiated, separate from campaign donations") but no UI, routing logic, or ToS language for voluntary tips exists. When the fee language is removed, something will need to fill that gap — at minimum a UI element and a ToS paragraph. This audit documents what to *remove*; the tip mechanism is a separate build task.

**2. The operations wallet concept still applies.** Even without a fee, the ops wallet (funded by tips + capital contributions) and its governance rules (surplus → community vote, no individual distribution) remain valid. The wallet itself doesn't go away — just its fee-based funding source does. Don't delete ops wallet language wholesale; update the funding-source description only.

**3. `GOVERNANCE.md` version bump is appropriate.** The fee elimination is a material change. A changelog entry (v0.8 → v0.9 or v1.0) documenting "Fee model eliminated; zero-fee pass-through adopted; operations funded by founder capital contributions and voluntary tips" is appropriate and expected.

**4. Attorney must review the new TOS_DRAFT.md §7.** The current draft's attorney review notes are keyed to the fee-based model. When §7 is rewritten, the attorney review flags for that section need to be updated accordingly.

**5. The `feeBufferPercent: 2` in campaigns.js is a network fee buffer, NOT a platform fee.** It stays (possibly renamed). Do not zero it out — Monero transactions have real network costs (~$0.01) and campaign goals should still include a small variance buffer.
